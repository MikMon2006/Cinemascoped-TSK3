LINK!!! PENNY'S DRESS!!!

Newline Cinema | Adam Shankman | 2007 | PG


Hairspray (2007) is a musical set in 1962 Baltimore, centred around Tracy Turnblad, an upbeat and ambitious teenager who dreams of dancing on The Corny Collins Show, a popular local TV program. Despite being judged for her size, Tracy earns a spot on the show thanks to her talent and confidence. Once on the show, Tracy becomes aware of the unfair segregation policies, Black dancers are only allowed to appear once a month. Outraged by this, she befriends Black students from her school and joins them in pushing for full integration. Tracy becomes a symbol of change, using her growing popularity to stand up for equality.

Her activism puts her at odds with the show’s producer, Velma Von Tussle, who wants to keep the show segregated and her daughter Amber in the spotlight. With the help of her friends, family, and fellow dancers, Tracy leads a protest that results in the show finally becoming integrated. The film ends on a high-energy note, celebrating themes of acceptance, social justice, and self-love through music, dance, and unity.
